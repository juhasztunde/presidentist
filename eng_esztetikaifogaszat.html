Aesthetic dentistry

In our civilised society, the role of dentistry goes beyond maintaining healthy teeth and plays an important role in achieving a high aesthetic need. Although the health of teeth has priority over all cosmetic requirements, now we have the opportunity to create a harmonious smile even if nature was not that generous with us in this regard. Let's see what a Hollywood smile really means!

Aesthetic dentistry is a generic term: a high level of implementation of different technologies with the aim to create the most harmonious image that perfectly suits the patient's face. Careful planning always precedes the implementation, where we discuss the patient’s needs and check the available options. Once the plan is complete, careful preparation can begin. We often prepare the teeth in advance with a magnifying glass or even a microscope, therefore it requires more time than a usual dental preparation. Parts of the technology can be linked to our office while the other parts are linked to a dental laboratory. In order to achieve the most favourable aesthetic effect, several consultations appointment may be needed. As a result, however, the perfect natural-looking aesthetically pleasing smile is available, which most of us desire.

Teeth Whitening

For those, who have been blessed with neat, well-arranged teeth and only dream of a little whiter teeth, we can offer various teeth whitening services. The most effective process is the dental whitening carried out in our office, where 2-3 shades of lightening can be achieved in about an hour. Whitening can be done at home as well, with a lower amount of active substance. In this case, an individual dental rack rail is created by the dental technician after an impression is taken.

Sometimes, teeth that have previously undergone root canal treatment can change colour over time and mismatch the colour of the surrounding teeth. In such a case, it is recommended to carry out teeth whitening only on the tooth in question.

Aesthetic Dental Filling

This is one of the most commonly used methods, but it is not the same as the widely known white filling. In the case of tooth decay, tooth construction or reparation of front teeth, we apply a special stratification technique. During this process, the layers of the tooth’s material of different translucency and colour are constructed separately with a special composite material designed for this purpose. This requires skill, time and a feel for aesthetics but it results in a perfect appearance that is extremely similar to the original status. Sometimes, even doctors are having a hard time noticing it! (see dental fillings)

Aesthetic Dental Inlay

If the loss of the tooth material is considerable, we have two solutions. Aesthetic inlays (inserts) are applied to still useable grinders. They are prepared by a dental technician in order to fill the cavity prepared by the doctor previously. The aim is, of course, again to match the other intact teeth in colour and form. (see: dental fillings)

Complete Dental Ceramic Crown

Another great solution to rebuild strongly destructed teeth is the complete ceramic crown. Since the metal that is used in general is missing in this case and the crown is entirely made of tooth-coloured material, the aesthetic effect is much higher and the result is more perfect. The aesthetics of the upper incisors always depend on how well they can mimic the light transmittance of the original tooth. The elimination of the metal increases the similarity to the natural teeth tremendously. We also choose this solution when we want to correct minor situation disorders or when we want to mask the discolouration in the area of front teeth when they underwent root canal treatment before. (see: dental restorations)

Shells

Shells are solutions used specifically for aesthetic purposes in the front tooth region to compensate for discrepancies in colour or position. Properly preserved teeth material and a good anatomical position of the incisors are a necessity in order to execute this beautiful, but very time-consuming restoration process.

Aesthetic Surgery

Often, even with nice-shaped, healthy teeth or well-positioned and well-ossified implants, our eyes are not satisfied with the final aesthetic results. In some cases, the gums around the teeth, the tissue quality or the location of the gums are responsible for this. This is the so-called "pink and white" aesthetics, which means a harmonious ratio of the flawless aesthetic tooth and the healthy gums surrounding it.

The correction or creation of such aesthetic is possible by using the sophisticated, meticulous tools of periodontal surgery.

This includes aesthetic disorders associated with the retraction of the gums. Correction of gums caused by non-dental disease, which may cause cold sensitivity, even combined with dental wear, is now a routine procedure.

Not only little or incomplete gums, but too thick gum or too much soft tissue can cause aesthetic problems too. A typical case of this is the so-called 'gum smile', where the ratio of teeth and gums is shifted towards the gums, resulting in a disharmonic overall picture. This disorder is easily noticed by a layman since the teeth are too small and gums are too visible when smiling. Aesthetic surgery can be also a solution to this problem.
