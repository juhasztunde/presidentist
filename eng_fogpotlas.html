Dental restorations

Crowns and Bridges

If the root part of a tooth is well fixed and healthy in the bone, but the crown part is incomplete, weakened, or needs aesthetic repair, a dental crown will be made to restore it. If necessary the tooth is preserved with root canal treatment and bar construction. After precise preparation, an impression is made, which will be cast into a model by a dental technician to obtain an exact replica of the oral cavity. According to traditional technology, the crown’s frame is shaped with wax and then moulded into metal/noble metal. This frame is covered with tooth-coloured ceramics, making it aesthetically similar to the surrounding teeth.

Today's technology allows us to construct a digitally designed frame using a computer for carving. Thus, there is a greater range of materials we can choose from, the crown can be made of different tooth-coloured materials.

Hidden Anchor Removable Replacements

If the position of the retained teeth in the jaw does not allow fixed replacements (bridges) to be made, then the removable parts that replace the missing teeth, shall be connected to the remaining teeth in a safe and stable manner. We secure it with invisible precision elements, so it's undetectable to both the patient and others when they eat and talk.

In this case, the patient’s own teeth are covered with a metal-ceramic crown (this includes anchoring elements). The removable part is a prosthesis made of plastic teeth placed on a metal frame.

Muscles of mastication and chewing function

In our office, we have a policy of looking at the oral cavity as a functional complex. This is always the main aspect of treatment. Therefore, the primary objective is to maintain or restore the chewing function and to preserve the health of the mastication muscles. In order to achieve this, we use state-of-the-art tools and methods to capture occlusion and articulation (biting). Our specialist in this matter helps to solve the already existing joint complaints.

Teeth grinding

Today's modern lifestyle often causes stress situations such as anxiety and tension accumulating and causing involuntary night-time constriction and grinding. This is often hidden from the individual, as it does not always cause subjective complaints. Most of the time, it is the sleep partners who draw attention to the problem. Teeth grinding may lead to problems with the mastication muscles and cause abnormal dental abrasion in the long term, which may significantly affect the quality of life over time. The solution for this is to heed the expert’s advice and to wear a bite-guard splint at night prepared by a dental technician.
