High-tech surgery

Guided implantation with 3D surgical template

When most people hear the word surgery, they instantly want to avoid the whole process as it seems quite hard and painful.

The answer is yes, it is indeed possible.

We are in such a fortunate position, as odontology is a field of medicine where the newest innovations of science first emerge and implemented.

As the 3D representation of the human body became a reality, the industry started using 3D printing simultaneously. Planning with high precision grew and based on the high accuracy plans, implantations could be placed without a surgical incision.

It is an undeniable advantage, that:

  • The duration of an operation halved
  • Neglecting the incision prevents the swelling of the surgical area
  • The chance to immediately use artificial dental roots rises
  • There are no sutures
  • The damage of anatomical structures are avoided, such as the nerve channels and the sinus, which could be the main causes of complications
  • The implantations lean on the biggest bone surface and are in the best position to carry the teeth

The template is prepared by a 3D printer to facilitate fast and risk-free medical work, it marks the CT planned position of the implants to assist the surgeon.

This is especially useful when the bone supplement is insufficient or the implants need to be burthen shortly after the surgery.

Also, specific services are available related to implantation systems or ones that are compatible with multiple implant systems. (For further information please visit the site of SMART GUIDE.)

3D CAD/CAM unique bone segment

Thanks to one of the latest developments in dental technology bone replacement, in case of larger bone deficits, is possible without removing a bone from the body of the patient (bone transplant). With a preliminary three-dimensional CT scan of the mandible, we can use a computer to virtually design the optimal bone shape. Sterilised, antigen-free human bone is carved into the desired shape using CAD/CAM technology. With a screw, the individual bone segment is fixed to the appropriate area of the jaw bone. After sufficient recovery time and removal of the fixing screws, it is possible to place an implant of sufficient size.

Ultrasound piezosurgery

Rapid and perfect healing is based on a minimal trauma operation or in other words, a minimal-invasive surgery. The piezoelectric surgical instrument is a revolutionary innovation that uses special ultrasonic energy, which creates the place of the artificial dental root in the bone without the usage of a drill. Its huge advantage is that it compresses the bone, but it doesn't take away any of it. It's extremely gentle with bone tissue and allows for sophisticated, delicate surgical separation, unlike any other. It is especially recommended, if:

  • the low bone density puts the implant’s stability at risk,
  • the narrow maxillary spine is not able to accommodate the implant.

Piezoelectric apparatus can be used in all kinds of bone interventions such as the sinus lifting operations or in certain periodontal surgeries.
