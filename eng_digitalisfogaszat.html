Digital dentistry

As computer technology is present in the industry and our everyday life, it is also a part of the modern dentistry and it facilitates faster and more precise execution. We have summarized the most important areas where this technology serves odontology.

Computer-controlled surgical design, implant and bone implantation

Excellent addition to the surgical experience that remains essential is the 3-dimensional image that we obtain with the help of CBCT. Using the CT image and software, we can create a surgical template that provides the intended position of the implant with the utmost certainty. We can pay attention to the most optimal placement for future dental replacement, the most suitable areas of bone reception, and the protection of important anatomical formulas from injury. With this, we can provide patients with opportunities such as intervention with less postoperative pain or immediate dental replacement after surgery. (see: high-tech surgery/navigated implantation .)

Computer design can be very helpful, even in the most difficult cases of bone replacement, where you want to avoid the unpleasant consequences of your own bone transplantation. The natural bone is fixed by screws and it fits precisely into the bone defect shown in the CT. The precise fit eliminates the need for a long and difficult process of carving, so the surgery is much faster, and a large fitting surface allows for safe healing. (see: high-tech surgery .)

Computer-controlled surgical design, implant and bone implantation

Not only the medical side, but the technical background responsible for the preparation of the dental implant has also undergone a significant change. The essential elements of dental laboratories, such as wax moulding and metal casting tools, which were used for decades, are slowly disappearing from the workspace of the modern lab. Most of the experts must have the necessary skills in the world of computer design and computer-controlled carving machines, as this is our present now.

Dental preparation is often done under a magnifying glass. In some cases, we use a mouth scanner in order to get an impression which is a must when preparing a dental implant. It can be used to transfer more accurate information and result than a high-precision analogue print. Digital or digitized data shall be designed on the computer screen with complete care, complete anatomy and accuracy. After this, the information is moulded into a dental replacement of different materials by the carving machine.

Of course, human contribution cannot be neglected in this case either, as the masking and painting of the ceramics are prepared by the hand of the dental technician. This often requires a sort of artistic skill, because machines can only contribute to this process partially.

Temporary Dental Implants

The precise design allows us to prepare a temporary fix replacement before the surgery. Therefore, after careful planning, the patient may leave with a temporary replacement designed and fixed for implants on the day of surgery.

In cases of certain treatments, temporary dental replacements are recommended for the duration of healing. Thus, a temporary replacement can be made for someone’s own teeth by computer design and a carving machine, which, thanks to digital design, is entirely the same as the final replacement will be.

Final Dental Implants

Unlike traditional technologies, computer design allows a more precise fit. Whether you’re talking about a crown or bridge designed for your own teeth or an implant-anchored dental work, with the help of the computers frames or complete ceramic replacements can be designed.
