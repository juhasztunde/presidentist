Root canal treatment

Root canal treatment is required if the internal tissue of the tooth has died due to infection, chemical or physical trauma. The reason for the treatment is often due to deep tooth decay, which is usually formed under the already filled area. Pain may occur, but this is not statutory, often the process can remain painless for a long time. The need for root canal treatment may arise during with dental intervention (e.g. filling replacement). For example, if we approach the tooth while filling during the removal of the cavity, it may affect the tooth in such a way that it can only be saved by root canal treatment. In rare cases, during the preparation of the tooth for a crown, we are forced to treat the root canal and do the filling for the same reason as mentioned above.

During the root canal treatment, the tissue in the tooth is removed in order to explore the entire inner cavity of the tooth: we roadmap all root canals and clean the insides both mechanically and chemically. This can be a one-off procedure when we remove the dead tissues and expand the root canals while filling them with the necessary filler. However, in cases where treatment was delayed for a long time, a single session does not resolve the existing infection condition. In this case, the root canal is filled with a disinfectant paste, not with the final filling material. The final root filling is prepared for the second or the third time.

Root-treated teeth are more fragile, therefore we need to pay extra attention to restore the satisfactory level of ability to chew. Since the root-treated teeth are usually heavily destructed, for treatment we go with taps, inserts or crowns instead of filling. If the infection has spread through the root tip to the surrounding bone and caused specific tissue changes, the root canal filling alone may not make the situation complain-free. In this case, we are talking about a surgical finish called root tip resection.
